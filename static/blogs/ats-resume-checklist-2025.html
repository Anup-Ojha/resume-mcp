ATS Resume Checklist 2025: 10 Things That Get Your Resume Rejected (Before a Human Reads It)

🗓 March 27, 2025· ResumeForge Team· ⏱ 7 min read· #ATSResume #ResumeChecklist #JobSearch2025

Here's a sobering number: 75% of resumes are rejected by an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) before a single human ever reads them. That means three out of four job seekers pour hours into crafting a resume — and it disappears into a black hole before it ever reaches a recruiter's desk.

The good news? ATS systems follow predictable rules. Once you know the rules, you can beat them every time. Here's the definitive 10-point checklist for 2025.

98.4% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS software to filter resumes. Source: Jobscan 2025

The 10 ATS Resume Killers (and How to Fix Each One)

1

Using Headers, Footers, and Text Boxes

ATS parsers often skip content inside headers, footers, and text boxes entirely. Your name, phone number, or key skills could be invisible to the system if placed in these areas.

✅ Fix: Put all critical info in the main body of the document
2

Missing the Job Title in Your Resume

Candidates who include the exact job title somewhere in their resume are 10.6x more likely to get an interview. ATS systems use semantic matching — if you're applying for "Senior Data Analyst" and your resume says "Data Specialist", you may score zero.

✅ Fix: Mirror the exact job title from the posting in your headline/summary
3

Using Images, Charts, and Graphics

That beautiful infographic resume you designed? An ATS sees it as a blank page. No ATS can read text embedded in images. Skill bars, profile photos, logos, and icons are all invisible.

✅ Fix: Plain text only. Save the design for a portfolio website link
4

Non-Standard Section Headings

ATS systems are trained to find sections called "Work Experience", "Education", "Skills". If you get creative ("Where I've Been", "My Journey"), the ATS won't categorize your experience correctly.

✅ Fix: Use standard headings: Work Experience, Education, Skills, Certifications
5

Keyword Stuffing (The 2025 Trap)

Old advice said to pack as many keywords as possible. Modern ATS systems in 2025 use semantic analysis and density scoring. A keyword appearing 15 times raises red flags. Worse, some ATS now detect and penalize obvious stuffing.

✅ Fix: Use each major keyword 1-3 times naturally in context
6

Saving as PDF When ATS Can't Read It

Not all PDFs are created equal. PDFs with embedded fonts or scanned pages can produce garbled text when parsed. Some older ATS systems still struggle with PDFs entirely.

✅ Fix: Use a LaTeX-generated or clean Word-to-PDF. Always test with a PDF reader. ResumeForge generates ATS-safe PDFs by default
7

Vague Bullet Points With No Numbers

"Responsible for managing a team" tells an ATS nothing. "Led a team of 8 engineers, reducing deployment time by 40%" includes keywords (led, team, engineers, deployment) and a quantifiable result that ATS scoring algorithms reward.

✅ Fix: Add numbers to every bullet: team size, % improvement, revenue, time saved
8

One-Size-Fits-All Resume

Sending the same resume to 100 jobs is the fastest way to get 100 rejections. Each job description is essentially a keyword map. Your resume must reflect the specific language of that posting.

✅ Fix: Tailor your resume for every application. Use ResumeForge's AI tailor tool — it takes under 5 minutes
9

Missing Skills Section

ATS systems specifically scan for a dedicated Skills section. Many are set up to extract skills into a separate field in the candidate database. Without this section, your skills may not be indexed properly.

✅ Fix: Always include a clearly labeled "Skills" or "Technical Skills" section
10

Fancy Fonts and Non-Standard Characters

Decorative fonts, special Unicode characters, and fancy bullet symbols can corrupt ATS parsing. What displays as ● on your screen might render as â— in the ATS database.

✅ Fix: Stick to standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Garamond) and standard bullet characters

The 2025 ATS Reality Check

The ATS landscape in 2025 is more sophisticated than ever. Systems like Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, and iCIMS now incorporate:

  • Natural Language Processing (NLP) — understanding context, not just keywords
  • Skills ontologies — mapping "JavaScript" to "JS" to "ECMAScript" automatically
  • AI ranking — scoring candidates on a 0-100 scale before a human reviews
  • Duplicate detection — flagging near-identical applications across the same company

📊 Key stat: 88% of employers believe ATS filters out qualified candidates. That means the system is imperfect — but knowing its rules still dramatically increases your chances of getting through.

Quick ATS Score Test

Before submitting any resume, do this 60-second check:

  1. Copy the job description's key skills and requirements
  2. Ctrl+F your resume for each keyword
  3. If fewer than 60% appear naturally in your resume, tailor it before submitting
  4. Check that your resume filename is clean: FirstName-LastName-Resume.pdf
  5. Make sure there are no tables, columns, or text boxes in the layout
