ATS Resume Checklist 2025: 10 Things That Get Your Resume Rejected (Before a Human Reads It)

RMResume-MCP Team March 27, 2025 7 min read #ATSResume2025 #ResumeChecklist #JobSearch2025

Here's a sobering number: 75% of resumes are rejected by an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) before a single human ever reads them. That means three out of four job seekers pour hours into crafting a resume — and it disappears into a black hole before it ever reaches a recruiter's desk.

The good news? ATS systems follow predictable rules. Once you know the rules, you can beat them every time. Here's the definitive 10-point checklist for 2025 — backed by Jobscan's latest research and the actual behavior of enterprise ATS platforms like Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, and iCIMS.

The 10 ATS Resume Killers

1

Using Headers, Footers, and Text Boxes

ATS parsers often skip content placed inside headers, footers, and floating text boxes entirely. Your name, phone number, email, or key skills could be completely invisible to the system if you've placed them in these areas.

✅ Fix: Put all critical information in the main body of the document — never in headers, footers, or text boxes
2

Missing the Job Title in Your Resume

Candidates who include the exact job title somewhere in their resume are 10.6x more likely to get an interview. ATS systems use semantic matching — if you're applying for "Senior Data Analyst" and your resume says "Data Specialist", you may score zero on title match.

✅ Fix: Mirror the exact job title from the job posting in your headline or summary section
3

Using Images, Charts, and Graphics

That beautiful infographic resume you designed? An ATS sees it as a blank page. No ATS can read text embedded inside images. Skill bars, profile photos, logos, icons, and decorative elements are all invisible to the parser.

✅ Fix: Plain text only. Save the design portfolio for your personal website link in the header
4

Non-Standard Section Headings

ATS systems are trained to find sections labelled "Work Experience", "Education", and "Skills". If you get creative with headings ("Where I've Been", "My Journey", "What I Know"), the ATS won't categorize your experience correctly and may skip those sections.

✅ Fix: Use standard headings only — Work Experience, Education, Skills, Certifications, Projects
5

Keyword Stuffing (The 2025 Trap)

Old advice said to pack as many keywords as possible. Modern ATS systems in 2025 use semantic analysis and keyword density scoring. A keyword appearing 15 times raises red flags. Worse, some ATS platforms now actively detect and penalize obvious keyword stuffing.

✅ Fix: Use each major keyword 1–3 times naturally within the context of your experience
6

PDF Format Issues

Not all PDFs are created equal. PDFs with embedded fonts, scanned images, or complex layouts can produce garbled or missing text when parsed by ATS. Some older systems still struggle with PDFs entirely and prefer .docx.

✅ Fix: Use a LaTeX-generated PDF (Resume-MCP's default) — these produce the cleanest, most ATS-reliable output of any format
7

Vague Bullet Points Without Numbers

"Responsible for managing a team" tells an ATS nothing. "Led a team of 8 engineers, reducing deployment time by 40%" includes high-value keywords (led, team, engineers, deployment) and a quantifiable result that ATS scoring algorithms actively reward.

✅ Fix: Add numbers to every bullet — team size, percentage improvement, revenue impact, time saved
8

One-Size-Fits-All Resume

Sending the same resume to 100 jobs is the fastest way to get 100 rejections. Each job description is essentially a keyword map the ATS uses to rank candidates. Your resume must reflect the specific language and priorities of each individual posting.

✅ Fix: Tailor every application — use Resume-MCP's AI tailor tool to do it in under 5 minutes per job
9

Missing Skills Section

ATS systems specifically scan for a dedicated Skills section and extract skills into a separate field in the candidate database. Without this section, your skills may not be indexed properly — even if they appear scattered throughout your work experience bullets.

✅ Fix: Always include a clearly labeled "Skills" or "Technical Skills" section near the top of your resume
10

Fancy Fonts and Non-Standard Characters

Decorative fonts, special Unicode characters, and fancy bullet symbols can corrupt ATS parsing. What displays as a beautiful bullet (●) on your screen might render as "â—" in the ATS database — breaking keyword matching entirely.

✅ Fix: Stick to standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Garamond) and standard ASCII bullet characters only
Key stat: 88% of employers believe ATS filters out qualified candidates. That means the system is imperfect — but knowing its rules still dramatically increases your chances of getting through.

Quick ATS Score Test

Before submitting any resume, run this 60-second check:

  1. Copy the job description's key skills and requirements into a text document
  2. Open your resume and use Ctrl+F to search for each keyword
  3. If fewer than 60% of the key terms appear naturally in your resume, tailor it before submitting
  4. Verify your resume filename is clean: FirstName-LastName-Resume.pdf
  5. Confirm there are no tables, multi-column layouts, or text boxes in the document structure
